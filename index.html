Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an iconic mausoleum located in Agra, India, and is widely regarded as a masterpiece of Mughal architecture. Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal, its construction began in 1632 and was completed in 1648, with some additional phases extending to 1653. The structure is primarily made of white marble, which reflects different hues throughout the day. The Taj Mahal features intricate inlay work, beautiful gardens, and a symmetrical layout, embodying a blend of Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and attracts millions of visitors from around the world each year. The love story behind its creation adds to its allure. Have you had a chance to see it, or is it on your travel list?

kuthub Minar

The Qutub Minar is a towering minaret located in Delhi, India. It stands at 73 meters (240 feet) and is made of red sandstone and marble. Construction began in 1193 under the rule of Qutb al-Din Aibak and was completed by his successor, Iltutmish. The Qutub Minar is adorned with intricate carvings and inscriptions in Arabic, and it serves as a prime example of Indo-Islamic architecture. The Billamandhir, also known as Billah Mandir or Billah Mandir, is a temple located in the eastern part of India, particularly in the state of West Bengal. It is dedicated to the worship of the deity Billah, a form of the goddess Durga.
